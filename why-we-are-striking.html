Why we are striking: United we stand, divided we fall ✊

Welcome to the virtual picket line. I have joined thousands members of the University and Colleges Union (UCU) in a strike at over 60 institutions nationally. [1,2]

As an employee, the personal decision to strike has not been an easy one. Withholding labour is the last resort when all other options have been exhausted. Striking is not an enjoyable activity for anyone, so I need to explain why I have joined the strike:

Two of the core functions of a University are:

  1. Teaching
  2. Research

Without these fundamental foundations, a University is not a University.

The University of Manchester, like many in the UK, has aspirations to be a world class University.

In order to achieve these goals, Universities need to recruit and retain the best teachers and researchers that they can.

To retain world class teachers and researchers, Universities need to provide fair pay and pensions (pensions are, in effect, deferred pay). However:

  1. A series of sub-inflation pay awards has meant that academics have endured a pay cut of 20% since 2009. During the same period the numbers of senior managers employed by the University on salaries in excess of £100k have increased dramatically. In 2018, 331 staff at the University of Manchester earned over £100k and 25 of those earned more than £150k.
  2. In 2011 we agreed (reluctantly) to changes in our Universities Superannuation Scheme (USS) pension scheme. These changes mean that the typical USS member will lose £240,000 in retirement. We have already made major sacrifices to preserve the USS scheme. The employers are demanding unnecessary further concessions.

The senior management of the University say there are "very real pressures on our limited resources" [3] In other words, they are saying that the money just isn’t there to meet these demands. This simply isn’t true. In 2018 the University of Manchester reported an operating surplus of £43m, plus cash reserves of over £315m, as well as net assets of £1.7 billion. Across the UK, Universities reported a combined surplus of £2.27 billion pounds in 2017. The money is there to improve staff pay and pensions. This is what our dispute is about.

The UCU would like to avoid strike action. They have announced the dates of planned action well in advance in order to give the employers a chance to negotiate properly and in good faith. If you support our cause, I would ask you to e-mail the University’s President and Registrar urging them to find a negotiated settlement to this dispute. It doesn’t have to be a long e-mail, I’d suggest something like:

Dear President/Registrar,

I would like to be taught by fairly paid staff. Please use your influence to persuade Universities UK and UCEA to negotiate an acceptable settlement.

Yours faithfully ...
